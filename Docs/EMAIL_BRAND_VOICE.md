# Behavior School Email Voice

Behavior School emails should sound like a useful note from Rob, not a campaign assembled by a marketing department.

## The Voice

- Write as a practicing school BCBA talking to one person.
- Be direct, calm, practical, and honest about whether the paid product is needed.
- Use concrete school and study details instead of broad claims.
- Name the next action clearly. Most emails need one primary CTA.
- Invite a reply when a real person can help.
- Sign marketing and nurture messages as Rob or Rob Spain, BCBA, IBA.

## Sentence Rules

- Prefer short paragraphs and ordinary words.
- Contractions are welcome when they sound natural.
- Use first person when Rob is making an observation or offering help.
- Do not claim personal knowledge of a recipient's feelings or situation.
- Do not manufacture urgency, scarcity, praise, or familiarity.
- Do not stack three abstract benefits into every sentence.

## Phrases We Do Not Use

- unlock your potential
- transform your journey
- game changer
- whether you are just starting or...
- in today's fast-paced world
- designed to empower
- take your skills to the next level
- seamless, robust, comprehensive, or revolutionary
- the honest truth is
- no fluff

Also avoid formulaic contrasts such as "not X, but Y" unless that is genuinely the clearest sentence.

## Product Voice

**Behavior Study Tools:** Reduce the decision to one manageable study action. Never shame inactivity. Tell users to remain on the free plan when paid access would not help yet.

**Transformation Program:** Write colleague to colleague. Use real school-system details: referral quality, FBA timelines, implementable BIPs, staff training, fidelity, and caseload systems.

**Behavior School resources:** Say what the resource contains, who it is for, and what to do with it. Skip inflated introductions.

## Final Read

Before an email is activated, read it aloud and check:

1. Could Rob say this naturally to a colleague or candidate?
2. Is there one specific reason this person is receiving it?
3. Is the next step obvious?
4. Is every product claim true?
5. Would the email still make sense without the button?
6. Does it avoid sounding like a template or an AI summary?
