## The Optimal Timing Strategy: 3-Pass System

### Pass 1: Answer Easy Questions (90-100 minutes)
**Goal:** Build momentum, bank time, boost confidence

**Strategy:**
- Read each question once
- If you know the answer immediately (within 20-30 seconds), select it
- If uncertain, **flag it and move on**
- Do NOT spend more than 45 seconds on any question

**What to Answer:**
- Definition questions (Domain A, B)
- Simple scenario questions
- Ethics questions with clear answers (Domain E)
- Any question where you're 80%+ confident

**What to SKIP:**
- Long scenario questions (save for Pass 2)
- Questions where you're torn between 2-3 answers
- Complex calculation or graph interpretation
- Anything that makes you think "hmm, I need to think about this"

**Target:** Complete 120-140 questions in 90-100 minutes

---

### Pass 2: Tackle Flagged Questions (80-100 minutes)
**Goal:** Solve challenging but solvable questions

**Strategy:**
- Review ALL flagged questions from Pass 1
- Spend 1.5-2.5 minutes per question
- Use process of elimination
- Apply the "Best Answer Rule" (explained below)
- Make educated guesses on some

**How to Approach Each Question:**
1. **Re-read carefully** (you may have missed a key word)
2. **Eliminate obviously wrong answers** (narrow to 2 choices)
3. **Apply test-taking strategies** (more below)
4. **Select your best answer**
5. **If still stuck after 2.5 minutes, make educated guess and move on**

**Don't:**
- Spend more than 3 minutes on any single question
- Second-guess questions you already answered (unless you have solid reason)
- Leave questions blank (no penalty for guessing)

**Target:** Answer 40-60 flagged questions

---

### Pass 3: Final Review & Educated Guesses (20-30 minutes)
**Goal:** Review answers, fill in remaining blanks, manage time

**Strategy:**
- Return to any unanswered questions
- Make educated guesses (never leave blank)
- Quick review of flagged questions if time permits
- Double-check you've answered all 185 questions

**Use Remaining Time For:**
1. Questions you're least confident about
2. Calculation double-checks
3. Reading comprehension verification
4. Making sure you didn't misclick

**Don't:**
- Change answers unless you have a specific reason
- Ruminate on questions you've already spent time on
- Rush so fast you misclick

## The "Best Answer Rule": When Multiple Answers Seem Right

### Problem
BCBA exam questions often have multiple "technically correct" answers. You need the **BEST** answer.

### The Hierarchy
When torn between answers, apply this decision tree:

1. **Most Effective Intervention**
   - Evidence-based > Anecdotal
   - Functionally-matched > Generic

2. **Least Restrictive**
   - Reinforcement-based > Punishment-based
   - Non-exclusionary > Exclusionary

3. **Most Ethical**
   - Respects client dignity
   - Involves stakeholders
   - Maintains professional boundaries

4. **Most Aligned with ABA Principles**
   - Data-driven > Intuition-based
   - Function-based > Topography-based
   - Conceptually systematic

### Example Application
**Question:** "A student engages in aggression to escape demands. Which intervention is BEST?"

**Options:**
A) Timeout after each aggressive episode
B) Teach functional communication (FCT) + extinction
C) Response cost system
D) Reduce task difficulty

**Analysis:**
- **A:** Punishment-based, doesn't teach replacement
- **B:** Function-matched (escape), teaches alternative, evidence-based âœ“
- **C:** Punishment, unclear how it addresses escape function
- **D:** Helpful but doesn't teach coping skills

**Best Answer: B** (Function-matched, reinforcement-based, teaches alternative)

## What to Do When You're Running Behind

### At 2-Hour Mark (Halfway)
**Check:** Have you answered at least 90-95 questions?

**If YES:** You're on pace. Continue 3-pass system.

**If NO (fewer than 85 answered):**
- **Speed up Pass 1:** Reduce decision time to 30 seconds
- **Be more aggressive with flagging:** If not 90% confident, flag it
- **Limit Pass 2 time:** Max 2 minutes per flagged question

### At 3-Hour Mark (75% complete)
**Check:** Have you answered at least 140 questions?

**If YES:** You're in good shape. Take your time on remaining questions.

**If NO (fewer than 130 answered):**
- **Triage mode:** Focus only on questions you can solve in 60-90 seconds
- **Make educated guesses:** Eliminate 1-2 options, guess, move on
- **Abandon Pass 3 review:** Just get through everything once

### With 30 Minutes Left
**Priority:** Ensure all 185 questions are answered (no blanks).

**If you have unanswered questions:**
1. Quickly read question stem (not scenarios)
2. Eliminate obviously wrong answers
3. Make educated guess
4. Move to next

**Don't:**
- Spend 10 minutes perfecting one answer
- Try to learn new content mid-exam
- Panic (stress hurts performance)

## Test-Taking Strategies for Better Performance

### Strategy 1: Read ALL Answer Choices
Even if Choice A looks perfect, read B, C, D. Sometimes D is "more perfect."

### Strategy 2: Flag Liberally in Pass 1
When in doubt, flag it. Better to return to 60 questions in Pass 2 than waste time agonizing in Pass 1.

### Strategy 3: Trust Your First Instinct (Usually)
Research shows first instincts are correct 70% of the time. Only change answers if you have a specific reason (misread question, remembered concept, etc.).

### Strategy 4: Eliminate "Absolute" Language
Answers with "always," "never," "all," or "none" are often wrong in applied fields like ABA.

**Example:**
- "Extinction ALWAYS produces an extinction burst" â† Likely wrong
- "Extinction OFTEN produces an extinction burst" â† More likely correct

### Strategy 5: Look for "BCBA-Speak"
Correct answers often use precise ABA terminology:
- "Function-based assessment" > "Figuring out why"
- "Differential reinforcement" > "Rewarding good behavior"
- "Operational definition" > "Clear description"

### Strategy 6: Match Function to Intervention
If the question describes behavior function (attention, escape, tangible, sensory), the correct answer should address that function.

**Example:**
- Behavior function: Escape from demands
- Best intervention: FCT for breaks + extinction of escape behavior
- Poor intervention: Timeout (doesn't teach escape alternative)

## Common Timing Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Spending 5+ Minutes on Single Question
**Problem:** One really hard question eats 5 minutes = 3-4 other questions sacrificed

**Solution:** 2.5-minute hard limit. Make educated guess, move on.

### Mistake #2: Not Flagging Questions
**Problem:** Trying to solve every question on first pass leads to time waste

**Solution:** Be ruthless with flagging. If not 80% confident, flag it.

### Mistake #3: Leaving Questions Blank
**Problem:** No partial credit, no penalty for guessing

**Solution:** ALWAYS guess. 25% random chance beats 0% blank.

### Mistake #4: Changing Answers Without Reason
**Problem:** Second-guessing yourself reduces accuracy

**Solution:** Only change if you:
- Misread the question
- Remembered a specific fact
- Made a calculation error

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long is the BCBA exam?
The BCBA exam is **4 hours (240 minutes)** with **185 multiple-choice questions**. This averages to about **1.3 minutes (77 seconds) per question**.

### Can I take breaks during the BCBA exam?
**No scheduled breaks.** The 4-hour timer runs continuously. You can raise your hand for restroom breaks, but the clock keeps running. Use the restroom before the exam starts.

### What if I don't finish all 185 questions?
Any unanswered questions are scored as incorrect. There's **no penalty for guessing**, so always select an answer even if you're unsure. Never leave questions blank.

### Should I spend equal time on each domain?
No. Spend more time on Domain G (24-26% of exam) and less on Domain A (8-10%). Budget your time proportionally to domain weight.

### How many practice exams should I take before the real exam?
Take **at least 2-3 full-length timed practice exams**. This builds stamina, tests pacing strategy, and reveals weak domains. Ideally, take practice exams weekly in the month before your test.

### What's the passing score for the BCBA exam?
The BACB uses a scaled score with a passing score of **400 out of 500**. This typically requires correctly answering **70-75% of scored questions** (approximately 122-131 correct out of 175 scored questions).
