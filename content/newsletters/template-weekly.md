# Weekly Email Template for School BCBAs

Use this as a working outline, not a fill-in-the-blanks marketing formula. Every email should be about one problem Rob has seen in school practice and one useful response.

## Subject

Name the actual topic. Examples:

- A cleaner way to triage new FBA referrals
- What I check before writing the BIP
- When staff data are too thin to make a decision

Avoid hype, vague benefits, and artificial curiosity.

## Draft

```text
Hi {{first_name}},

[Start with the concrete school situation in two or three sentences. Do not claim to know how the reader feels.]

[Explain what Rob does in that situation and why. Include enough detail that the reader can try it without buying anything.]

Try this this week:

1. [Specific action]
2. [Specific action]
3. [Specific action]

[One sentence linking to a relevant worksheet, article, or tool.]

[Single CTA]

If you try it, reply and tell me what happened.

Rob Spain, BCBA, IBA
Behavior School
```

## Editorial Check

- One real school problem
- One practical method
- One primary CTA
- No invented reader story or testimonial
- No generic encouragement
- No claims that cannot be supported
- Reads naturally aloud
