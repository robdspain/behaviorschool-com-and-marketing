# What Your BCBA Mock Exam Score Really Means

If you just scored 60% on a BCBA mock exam and felt your stomach drop, take a breath. You're not alone, and you're probably more prepared than you think.

## The Mock Exam Panic Cycle

Here's what happens to most BCBA candidates:

1. You take your first mock exam
2. You score somewhere between 50-70%
3. You panic, convinced you'll fail
4. You consider postponing your exam date
5. You question whether you're cut out for this field

**But here's what the data shows:** Many candidates who score in the 50-70% range on mock exams go on to pass the actual BCBA exam on their first attempt.

## Why Mock Exams Feel Harder

Mock exams—especially from certain popular providers—are often **intentionally more difficult** than the actual exam. Here's why:

### They're designed to overprepare you
If you can handle harder questions in practice, the real exam feels more manageable. It's like training at high altitude—when you come back down to sea level, everything feels easier.

### They test edge cases
Mock exams often include more obscure content and tricky wording than you'll encounter on test day. The actual BACB exam focuses on core competencies that practicing BCBAs need to know.

### They're not calibrated the same way
The BACB exam uses psychometric calibration to ensure consistent difficulty and fairness. Mock exams from third-party providers don't go through the same rigorous validation process.

## What Matters More Than Your Score

### Improvement over time
One mock score is a snapshot. What matters is the **trajectory**. If you scored 55% three weeks ago and 68% this week, you're trending in the right direction.

### Error analysis
Did you miss questions because you didn't know the content, or because you misread the question? The latter is much easier to fix.

### Content area patterns
The BACB reports scores by content area (Foundations, Applications, etc.). If you're consistently weak in one area, target that section rather than worrying about your overall percentage.

## The Exam Day Reality Check

The actual BCBA exam is **not designed to trick you**. The BACB wants competent, qualified BCBAs entering the field. Questions test whether you can:

- Apply ABA principles in realistic scenarios
- Make ethical decisions
- Prioritize interventions appropriately
- Collaborate with families and teams

If you've studied the content, practiced application, and developed test-taking skills, your mock exam scores are probably *underselling* your readiness.

Your mock exam score is **one data point**, not a prophecy. It's a tool to guide your studying, not a judgment of your worth as a future BCBA.

## Frequently Asked Questions About BCBA Mock Exam Scores

### Is 60% on a BCBA mock exam good enough to pass?

Yes, many candidates who score 60-70% on mock exams pass the actual BCBA exam. Mock exams are often intentionally harder than the real thing. Focus on improvement trends and content area analysis, not just your overall percentage.

### How many mock exams should I take before the BCBA exam?

Take at least 2-3 full-length mock exams spaced throughout your study period. Use the first as a diagnostic, the middle one(s) to track progress, and the final one 1-2 weeks before exam day to confirm readiness.

### What if my mock exam scores aren't improving?

If scores plateau or decline, change your study strategy. Shift from passive review (rereading notes) to active recall (practice questions, teaching concepts). Focus on your weakest content areas and analyze WHY you're missing questions, not just WHAT you're missing.

### When should I schedule my BCBA exam based on mock scores?

Schedule when you're consistently scoring 60%+ on mock exams, your scores are trending upward, and you can explain why wrong answers are wrong. If you're below 50% or scores are declining, extend your study timeline.

### Are all BCBA mock exams equally predictive?

No. Some providers create intentionally harder exams to overprepare candidates. The BACB doesn't publish official practice exams, so third-party mocks vary in difficulty and accuracy. Use multiple sources and focus on learning from mistakes, not just scoring high.
